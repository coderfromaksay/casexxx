Solar System Research

Welcome to an interactive journey through our solar system! Hover over a planet to learn more about it.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun. Its surface is covered with craters, and temperatures can reach 430°C during the day and -180°C at night.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, known for its dense atmosphere and extremely high surface temperatures reaching 462°C.

Earth

Earth is our home, the third planet from the Sun. It's the only known planet with life, thanks to the presence of liquid water and oxygen in its atmosphere.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun, known for its reddish color. It has the highest volcano in the Solar System - Olympus Mons.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. It's a gas giant with many moons and the famous Great Red Spot - a giant storm.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun, famous for its impressive rings. It's the least dense planet in the Solar System.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third largest. It's unique in that it rotates 'on its side' - its axis is tilted almost 90 degrees.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet in the Solar System. It's an ice giant with strong winds that can reach speeds of 2,100 km/h.

Exoplanets

Kepler-186f

Kepler-186f is the first Earth-size planet discovered in the potentially 'habitable zone' around another star.

TRAPPIST-1e

TRAPPIST-1e is one of seven Earth-size planets orbiting the ultra-cool dwarf star TRAPPIST-1.

Proxima Centauri b

Proxima Centauri b is an exoplanet orbiting in the habitable zone of the red dwarf star Proxima Centauri, the closest star to the Sun.
